# Product Strategy Brief

**Description:** 
A framework for transforming a list of product goals into a sharp, competitive, executive-ready strategy. It merges rigorous market analysis with an actionable document structure.

**When to use:**
- When a team presents a "strategy" that is actually just a list of goals or features.
- When drafting a new strategy brief for executive review.
- When you need to outmaneuver competitors, vendor lock-in, or market fragmentation.

**Core Principles:**
1. **Goals ≠ Strategy:** Goals are the destination (e.g., "Increase adoption"). Strategy is the opinionated, competitive path to overcome market gravity to get there.
2. **Exec-Friendly Framing:** Avoid academic frameworks (like Rumelt's "Diagnosis/Guiding Policy"). Speak the language of leadership: "Market Context" and "How We Win."
3. **Map the Real Battlefield:** Look beyond direct 1:1 competitors. Identify:
   - *Fragmentation:* Where is the ecosystem fractured?
   - *Vendor Bundling:* Who is rolling up features to force lock-in?
   - *Framework Defaults:* What is the developer's path of least resistance?
   - *Neo-Analysts:* Who is redefining the evaluation criteria?
4. **Phased Leverage:** Sequence the strategy to build trust first. Establish a neutral, undeniable baseline (Milestone 1) before dropping the hammer with your unique differentiator (Milestone 2).
5. **Offensive FAQs:** Use the FAQ section to tackle the most dangerous strategic landmines head-on (e.g., "What if our competitor benchmarks higher?").

**AI Instructions:**
When asked to write, review, or evaluate a strategy, the AI must strictly enforce the principles above and format the output using the following structure:

### 1. Market Context & Challenges
*Do not just list competitors. Describe the market dynamics, the fragmentation, the bundling motions, and the shifting goalposts (e.g., new analyst criteria). What is the specific mess we are stepping into?*

### 2. How We Win (The Strategic Thesis)
*This is the pivot. State clearly how we will cut through the noise. Shift the battleground to our advantage. (e.g., "Achieving SOTA performance is table stakes. We win by shifting the battleground to full-stack, real-world applicability.")*

### 3. Execution Pillars
*Translate the thesis into 3-4 tactical, coherent actions. How does this strategy manifest in the actual product roadmap or GTM motion?*

### 4. Phased Rollout (Milestones)
*Sequence the strategy. 
- **Milestone 1:** Establish the baseline / Build trust.
- **Milestone 2:** Introduce the core differentiator / Monetize.*

### 5. Strategic Risks & Mitigations
*Identify the biggest material risk to the strategy (e.g., a competitor outperforming us) and provide a concrete mitigation plan (e.g., algorithmic transparency).*

### 6. Strategic FAQ
*Draft hard-hitting questions that executives or skeptics will ask, and provide direct, unvarnished answers.*
