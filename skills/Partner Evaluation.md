# The PACT Rubric for Partner Evaluation

When meeting with potential partner companies at conferences and industry events, you need a quick, easy-to-remember framework to separate the signal from the noise. The **PACT** rubric helps quickly assess whether a company is worth a deep strategic partnership, a loose integration, or should be avoided entirely.

## P - Problem Fit
**Does their offering genuinely solve a meaningful problem for our shared users?**
- **Strong Fit:** They solve a painful problem that our customers complain about, but which is strictly outside our core competency.
- **Red Flag:** Their solution is a "nice-to-have" vitamin rather than a painkiller, or it overlaps with what we intend to build (Core vs. Context).

**Quick Screening Questions:**
- *"What specific customer pain point does your product solve better than anyone else?"*
- *"Is this something our users ask us for but we don't plan to build?"* (Ask yourself)

## A - Alignment
**Are our strategic goals and business models aligned?**
- **Strong Fit:** Our incentives are perfectly aligned. If they grow, we grow. Our target audiences overlap but our core value props don't compete.
- **Red Flag:** We are on a collision course. Their long-term roadmap expands directly into our territory, or our partnership would just be a temporary stepping stone for them.

**Quick Screening Questions:**
- *"Who is your ideal customer profile, and how does it overlap with ours?"*
- *"What does success look like for your company in 2-3 years?"* (Checking for overlapping roadmaps/expansion into our space)

## C - Complexity
**How difficult will it be to integrate or go-to-market with them?**
- **Strong Fit:** They have clean APIs, good documentation, and a clear, turnkey partner program. Low operational and technical overhead.
- **Red Flag:** Partnering would require deep, bespoke engineering work, heavy legal negotiations, or massive operational support. High effort for uncertain reward.

**Quick Screening Questions:**
- *"What does a typical integration look like for your partners, and how long does it take?"*
- *"Do you have public API documentation and a dedicated developer staging environment?"*
- *"Does this require bespoke engineering work from us, or can we plug-and-play?"* (Ask yourself)

## T - Trust & Traction
**Do they have real momentum and a reliable team?**
- **Strong Fit:** They have proven traction (real customers, good retention), and the team is transparent, responsive, and shares our cultural values.
- **Red Flag:** They are all hype/vaporware. They promise the world but don't have the engineering velocity or customer validation to back it up. 

**Quick Screening Questions:**
- *"Can you share a recent success story with a shared (or similar) customer?"*
- *"What are the key metrics you use to measure customer health and retention?"*
- *"Can you introduce us to an existing integration partner of yours?"*

---

### Evaluation Outcomes
After running a company through the PACT rubric, categorize them into one of three buckets:

1. **Partner Closely:** Strong Problem Fit, High Alignment, Low Complexity, High Trust. (Allocate engineering/GTM resources).
2. **Maintain Loose Orbit:** Good Problem Fit, but maybe high complexity or unproven traction. (Stay in touch, maybe do a lightweight co-marketing push, but hold off on deep technical integration).
3. **Avoid:** Poor Alignment (future competitor), High Complexity with low ROI, or low Trust. (Politely pass and do not allocate resources).
