# Blog Post Exploration & Drafting

## When to use
Activate when the user wants to pitch, draft, or refine a blog post or strategic exploration piece. This skill ensures posts are high-impact, rigorous, and tightly integrated with the existing knowledge base.

## Required Elements of the Pitch
The initial pitch must define:
- **Title:** Working title.
- **Topic:** The core concept.
- **Target Audience:** Who is this for?
- **Key Takeaway:** What will the reader learn?
- **Rationale ("Why Now?"):** Why is this an important story to tell today?
- **Call to Action (CTA):** What should the reader do next?
- **Key AI/Modern Themes:** How does this intersect with modern tech trends (e.g., the agentic era)?

## Drafting & Synthesis Rubric
When expanding the pitch into a draft, ensure the following elements are present:

1. **Rigorous Citation & Sources:**
   - **Primary Citations:** Link directly to original announcements, source articles, or datasets.
   - **External Resources:** Provide a "Curated Resources" section at the end with links to official pages (e.g., doctrine, courses, documentation) to encourage deeper reading.

2. **Cross-Domain Contextualization:**
   - Look for lessons, analogies, or principles in other disciplined fields (e.g., history, military organization, physical sciences) to give the piece a unique and authoritative perspective.

3. **Doctrinal & Operational Frameworks:**
   - Avoid abstract theory. Reference concrete, structured methodologies (e.g., planning formats, frameworks, SOPs) that back up the concept and make it actionable.

4. **Knowledge Vault Integration (Obsidian):**
   - **Internal Linking:** Search the vault for related internal notes and insert Obsidian-style wiki-links (`[[Note Name]]` or `[[Note Name#Section]]`).
   - **Build on Vault Concepts:** Integrate existing internal models, leadership case studies, or strategy maxims to ensure knowledge compound interest.

5. **Nuance & Critical Caveats:**
   - Explicitly state the limitations of any analogies or models. Explain where principles do *not* apply or how they could be misapplied in a different context.

## Actions
- **Pitching:** Help the user fill out the "Pitch" checklist first.
- **Drafting:** For each main point, search both the web (for external sources/citations) and the local vault (for internal cross-references) to enrich the draft.
- **Refining:** Before presenting the final draft, review the text to ensure it has:
  1. High-quality external links.
  2. Internal Obsidian vault links.
  3. A dedicated "Caveat" section highlighting limits of the core analogy.
  4. An "Official Resources" section at the end.
